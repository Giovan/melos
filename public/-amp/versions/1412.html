Embéo e Majaró Lucas 1837 (CALO1837)

Romani: Calo (Iberia) - Romani: Caló (Iberia)

Ocóna embéo lo chibó en Calo-romano George Borrow, lacró e Plastañi Biblica, andré o foros de Badajoz opré a mixa de Laloró, chaomo e berji de Jesunvais de 1836.



El Evangelio segun S. Lucas, traducido al Romaní, ó dialecto de los Gitanos de España.



In 1836 the Gospel of Luke was translated into Caló (Iberian Romani) by BFBS agent, George Borrow (1803-1881) with the help of Gypsies in Spain and Portugal. It was printed by the British and Foreign Bible Society in 1837.



Caló is the form of Romani spoken by Gitano Gypsies in Iberia (Spain and Portugal) and communities descended from them in Brazil. This was the first book of the Bible ever translated into any Romani language, and the first book ever published in any Romani language. This gospel was digitised frorm an original copy at the British and Foreign Bible Society (BFBS) library for the Uinited Bible Socities (UBS) Romani Affinity Group digitisation project.
