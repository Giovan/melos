Romani: Baltic

Baltic Romani
Baltic Romani is the form of Romani spoken in northern Poland and in the Baltic states of Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania.

Gospel of John in Lettish Romani
Janis Lejmanis (Mr Lehman) was a Lettish (or Latvian) Gyspy in the Republic of Latvia. He was a Christian from the Orthodox Church. In 1932 Lehman translated the Evangelīben māro raskīro Iisusko Hristoskīro Joannostir Čhindlo (Gospel of Jesus Christ According to John) from Russian and Latvian, two other languages he knew as well as Romani. He was recommended to the British and Foreign Bible Society (BFBS) by the Orthodox Archbishop John of Riga and the Lutheran Bishop Grinberg of Riga. The text was written in Latin script using Latvian orthography. Mr Anderson who worked for the British and Foreogn Bible Society in Riga had the translation checked by an educated Gypsy in Latvia who worked as a teacher. The text was also checked in Cambridge by the Scottish scholar Sir Donald MacAlister (1854-1934), a linguist who reputedly spoken 19 languages including Romany. The text was published by Britanijas un Ārzemju Bībeles Biedrība (British and Foreign Bible Society) in Riga in 1933. It was catalogued by BFBS as St John's Gospel in Lettish Romany.

This text was digitised from an original copy in the British and Foreign Bible Society archives, with the help of Mission Assist. The text was placed on line for the United Bible Societies (UBS) Romani Affinity Group digitisation project.
